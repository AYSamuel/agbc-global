Our story

Amazing Grace Bible Church began in Glasgow and has grown into a family spread across nations, joined by one grace and one Lord.

What we believe

The Bible is our foundation, Jesus is our Saviour, and the Holy Spirit is our helper. We gather to worship, grow, and carry one another.

Our mission

To reach every city God sends us to, and to make every member feel at home in the family, wherever they are in the world.

The Grace Awakening

Chapter 4 · Grace that finds you

Grace does not wait at the finish line for you to arrive. It runs out to meet you while you are still a long way off, still rehearsing the speech you think you need to earn your way back in.

That is the scandal at the centre of the gospel. Not that God loves the improved version of you, but that he loved you first, before a single thing was fixed, and called you his own.

So today, before you do anything, before you pray a better prayer or keep a better promise, hear this: you are already found. The searching is over. What remains is simply to be carried home.

Day 8
Today's verse
But God commends his own love toward us, in that while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.
Romans 5:8 (WEB)
Reflection

Grace doesn't wait for you to get it right. Before a single thing was fixed in you, love had already moved toward you, and made its home with you.

You are not loved because you performed. You are loved, and out of that love you get to live. Let that reorder your day.

Prayer
Father, thank you that I am loved before I perform. Help me rest in that today, and love others from the same grace. Amen.

The Grace Awakening

Chapter 4 · Grace that finds you

Grace does not wait at the finish line for you to arrive. It runs out to meet you while you are still a long way off, still rehearsing the speech you think you need to earn your way back in.

That is the scandal at the centre of the gospel. Not that God loves the improved version of you, but that he loved you first, before a single thing was fixed, and called you his own.

So today, before you do anything, before you pray a better prayer or keep a better promise, hear this: you are already found. The searching is over. What remains is simply to be carried home.

This is where rest begins. Not in the striving, not in the proving, but in the quiet confidence that the God who started this will be the one to finish it.
