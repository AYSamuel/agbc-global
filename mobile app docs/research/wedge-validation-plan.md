# User Research: Wedge Validation Plan

Validating the "belonging made visible" wedge (Family: testimonies + prayer loop + global map) before it is built in Phase 2.

## 1. Research Goal & Questions

**The question we are trying to answer:**
Does "belonging made visible" (testimonies + the prayer -> answered -> testimony loop + the global family map) match how AGBC members really experience staying connected and sharing faith across the diaspora, enough that it becomes the reason they open the app, rather than a nice-to-have on top of watch / give / events?

**What we think we know (assumptions to validate or challenge):**
1. Members feel real distance from their "home" branch and the wider family, and want that gap closed.
2. Members want to post testimonies publicly and read others' (rather than feeling exposed or keeping faith private).
3. The prayer -> answered -> testimony arc matches how they actually experience and talk about answered prayer.
4. Seeing the family alive across nations is emotionally meaningful, not a gimmick.
5. This matters more to them than the table-stakes features. The wedge really is the wedge.

**What we don't know (the gaps driving this research):**
- How members stay connected to the church family today when they are apart (WhatsApp groups, calls, Instagram, nothing), and what frustrates them about it.
- Whether public sharing creates hesitation, and how that differs across UK, Germany, Netherlands, and Nigeria cultures.
- Whether a public "Glory to God" reaction feels authentic or performative.
- What would actually pull a member to open the app on an ordinary weekday.
- For visitors: how they check out a church before showing up, and whether a public testimonies/prayer wall feels welcoming or off-putting to an outsider.

**Decision rule (set this BEFORE interviewing, so the research is honest):**
Decide in advance what result keeps, adjusts, or cuts the wedge. Proposed default (confirm or change):
- **Keep as specced** if most participants describe an unmet belonging/connection need in their own words AND react warmly to sharing testimonies publicly.
- **Adjust** (for example: prayer loop yes, public map later; or private-to-branch first) if the connection need is real but public sharing raises consistent hesitation.
- **Cut / re-think** if members mostly do not feel disconnected, or sharing faith publicly is something they actively avoid.

## 2. Participant Profile

**The three groups and why each is here:**

| Group | Who | Why we talk to them | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| A. Members far from "home" | Diaspora members who moved away from their original branch / country | The heart of the belonging thesis. If the need is real, it is loudest here. | 5-6 (weight the sample here) |
| B. Active local members | Regulars at their current branch | Tests whether the wedge matters even when you are not far from home. | 4-5 |
| C. Visitors / seekers | New, exploring, or unchurched people who have engaged with AGBC recently | Tests the "front door for visitors, no account wall" claim. | 3-4 |

**Ideal participant characteristics:**
- A mix of ages and tenure (not only the most active volunteers, who over-index on enthusiasm).
- At least a few who attend mostly online or rarely, not only front-row regulars.
- For Group A: people who genuinely relocated (Nigeria -> Europe, or between European cities).
- For Group C: recent first-time visitors or online viewers who are not yet committed.

**How we're recruiting them:**
- Berlin (Ayo) + Glasgow (HQ, English, the founder's branch): the cleanest cross-branch signal for the diaspora-in-Europe thesis.
- Pastors personally invite from their own knowledge of the congregation, using the screener below to fill each group. Avoid recruiting only friends; ask leaders to nominate a range.
- Warm intro message (below), then schedule 30 minutes.

**Number of participants:** 12-15 total. This is qualitative: you are looking for repeated patterns, not statistical significance. Stop a group once two or three interviews in a row surface nothing new (saturation).

## 3. User Interviews

**Golden rule:** ask about past behaviour, not hypothetical future behaviour. "Tell me about the last time you..." beats "Would you ever...?". Do not describe the app or the Family features until the optional concept reaction at the very end.

### Facilitator brief (read before running any interview)
- You are not selling or testing them. You are learning. Stay curious, not defensive.
- Do not pitch the app. Do not lead ("wouldn't it be nice if..."). If they ask what you are building, say "a new church app, and we want to get it right, so first we want to understand your real experience."
- Follow the energy: when they light up or hesitate, ask "tell me more about that."
- Embrace silence. Let them fill it.
- Offer the interview in the person's stronger language (DE / NL) where you can. Note the language used.
- Record only with consent. Take notes in the grid even if recording.

### Introduction script (read to every participant)
> "Thanks for your time. We're building a new app for the church, and before we build the wrong thing we want to understand your real experience of being part of the AGBC family. This isn't a test and there are no right or wrong answers. I'd love your honest opinion, including the critical bits. Is it OK if I record this just for our notes?"

### Warm-up (2-3 mins)
- Tell me a bit about you, and how you came to be part of AGBC.
- Where do you think of as your "home" branch, and where are you now?
- How often are you at a service these days, and how do you usually attend (in person or online)?

### Core questions (15-20 mins)

**A. Belonging and staying connected (tests assumption 1)**
- Walk me through the last time you felt really connected to the church family. What was happening?
- Now the opposite: the last time you felt out of the loop or distant from the family. What happened?
- When you are not at a service, how do you keep up with what is happening in the church and with people in it? (probe: WhatsApp, calls, Instagram, nothing)
- (Group A) Your original branch or people back home: how do you stay part of that now that you are here? What is hard about it?

**B. Testimonies and sharing faith (tests assumptions 2 and 3)**
- Tell me about the last testimony you heard or shared. Where did it happen, and how did it feel?
- When something good happens that you would credit to God, who do you tell, and how? Walk me through the last time.
- Have you ever held back from sharing something like that more widely? What stopped you?
- Tell me about the last time you asked others to pray for you, or prayed for someone else's request. How did you find out if it was answered? Did you go back and tell people it was answered?

**C. The wider family across nations (tests assumption 4)**
- Do you know what is happening at the other AGBC branches in other countries? If you wanted to find out, how would you? Do you care to?
- Tell me about a time you felt part of something bigger than your local congregation.

**D. What pulls them to an app (tests assumption 5)**
- Which apps do you open every day almost without thinking? What makes those stick?
- Do you use any church or faith apps now? Walk me through the last time you opened one and what for.
- (later, lightly) If AGBC had an app, what is the first thing you would hope to do with it?

**Group C (visitors/seekers) swap-ins:** replace the AGBC-history warm-up and Group A question with:
- Think about the last time you checked out a new church or a faith event. What did you do to decide whether to go? What made you come back, or not?
- If you landed on a church's app or page and saw members posting personal testimonies and prayer requests publicly, what would go through your mind?

### Optional concept reaction (final 5 mins only, AFTER all discovery)
Only now, briefly describe the idea in plain words: "One part of the app is a shared family space: members post short testimonies of what God has done, others tap 'Glory to God' to celebrate, you can post a prayer request and later mark it answered and turn it into a testimony, and there is a map showing the family is alive across all our nations."
- What is your gut reaction?
- What would make you actually use it? What would stop you?
- What, if anything, worries you about it?
(Keep this last so it cannot bias the honest discovery above. Label these notes clearly as "concept reaction" in the grid.)

### Closing
- Anything about staying connected to the church family that we didn't ask but matters to you?
- Is there someone else you think we should talk to?

## Screener (classify recruits into the right group)

Ask (or have the pastor answer) before scheduling:
1. How did you first become connected to AGBC, and roughly how long ago?
2. Which branch do you consider "home", and which city do you live in now? (Group A if these differ across cities/countries; else B)
3. In a typical month, how often are you at a service (in person or online)?
4. Are you comfortable with a relaxed 30-minute conversation about your experience of the church, recorded for our notes only?
5. (To spot visitors for Group C) Would you say you are settled here, still exploring, or fairly new to AGBC?

Aim to fill: 5-6 in Group A, 4-5 in Group B, 3-4 in Group C.

---

## Recruiting message (pastor sends, warm and personal)

> "Hi [name], we're building a new app for the AGBC family and I'd love your help shaping it. Before we build anything, we're having short, relaxed chats with members and visitors about their real experience of staying connected to the church. No prep needed, no tech knowledge required, and your honest opinion (including the critical bits) is exactly what we want. It takes about 30 minutes, in person after a service or on a video call, whenever suits you. Would you be up for it in the next couple of weeks?"

Optional: a small thank-you (a coffee, a book from the store) lifts turnout and signals you value their time. Confirm if you want to offer one.
